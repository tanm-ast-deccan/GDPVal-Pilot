Task prompt — delivered verbatim to the model

A mid sized FMCG manufacturer operating in the personal care category currently sources 100% of its critical raw material, Active Ingredient X, from an overseas supplier, Supplier A. You are required to compute and compare the total annual cost under two sourcing strategies: continuing 100% Supplier A, or introducing Supplier B under a 50–50 allocation. The analysis must calculate landed cost per unit, defect related quality cost per unit, working capital cost based on the RBI Repo Rate as set by the RBI Monetary Policy Committee resolution dated 8 April 2026, published on the official Reserve Bank of India website (rbi.org.in), and inventory carrying cost (computed on the supplier-specific lead time as the holding period) validated against government published FMCG benchmarks. You must calculate total effective cost per unit and total annual cost under each strategy. The decision must be purely cost driven and supported by transparent calculations. Only the three provided internal data files may be used for operational inputs. The only permitted external source is the RBI Repo Rate from the RBI MPC resolution dated 8 April 2026. No other assumptions, market data, or third-party sources may be introduced. The final deliverable must be prepared in the form of a formal Board Memo of approximately 3 to 5 pages. The memo must include an executive summary clearly stating whether Supplier B should be introduced or not, a breakdown of cost components for each sourcing strategy, a comparison table showing total per unit and annual costs, and a final recommendation explicitly marked as Accept or Reject for introducing Supplier B, and strategic considerations such as effect on defect rates, etc.

Step 5

Step 7
