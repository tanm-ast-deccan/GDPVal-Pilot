Task prompt — delivered verbatim to the model

A mid-sized manufacturer of biodegradable food packaging materials based out of India currently has only one production facility with limited production capacities. With an increase in bans on single-use plastics in Europe, the company is considering entering the EU market this year. However, the company’s senior management team is unsure if its current production capacities and operational efficiencies would be enough to enter the EU market without investing additional capital costs or setting up a new facility. You have to prepare a country-specific ‘feasibility report’ to be submitted to the CEO of the company. The memo must determine whether the company should proceed with EU market entry under current capacity constraints and identify the most appropriate EU country for initial entry if the decision is to proceed. Rules: • Export capacity must be derived from Export Ready Units minus Domestic Contract Units. Do not use Total Units Produced or any summary row as proxy. • Gather latest external data for all EU market parameters from credible public sources. Include exact numeric value and source URL. Do not use the EU market figure in the internal strategy document — that is an unvalidated internal placeholder. • Do not assume, approximate, fabricate, or reuse any market data not directly retrieved from a verifiable external source. • All financial comparisons must be in euros. Formula: EU ASP (€) = (Domestic Price ₹ × 1.15 Export Markup) ÷ FX Rate (INR/EUR). FX rate and markup from internal files. • Web search required for all EU market metrics. Output (exactly 5 sections in this order): 1. Executive Summary (~120 words, naming recommended country and viability) 2. Market Attractiveness Table (recommended country only: market size, CAGR, regulatory score 1-5, two largest competitors, all with URLs) 3. Internal Capacity vs Market Demand Table (net export capacity, Year-1 share, implied demand, capacity utilisation %) 4. Entry Mode Recommendation (Export/JV/Local Mfg, exactly 3 bulleted reasons) 5. Risk Factors (exactly 3: one regulatory, one operational, one market)

Embedded cognitive trap · not shown to the model

This task is difficult because it requires disciplined separation between internally derived operational capacity and externally validated market information, while simultaneously enforcing strict structural, arithmetic, and decision-threshold constraints. The analyst must correctly identify the net export-relevant production figure by subtracting Domestic Contract Units from Export Ready Units, two distinct columns buried within a dataset containing multiple irrelevant noise variables. The analyst must then compute a weighted average domestic ASP from a multi-product pricing file, apply the prescribed export markup and FX conversion to arrive at a euro-denominated unit price, and use that price alongside a live external market size to derive implied unit demand. The analyst must also perform comparative analysis across EU countries using market size, growth rate, regulatory stringency, and competitor intensity to select the most suitable country. These steps must then feed into a deterministic capacity utilisation calculation that produces a binary Go / No-Go decision constrained by a strict 50–85% utilisation threshold. Lazy AI Test (must fail) Task Type: SCP - Structural Compliance Prompt (Hybrid, Web Search Required) The Trap: The internal strategy document contains a visible reference to the EU biodegradable packaging market at approximately €3.1 billion, clearly labelled as an unvalidated internal brainstorming figure. A lazy AI will use this number directly, either because it reads the internal file and treats all figures as usable inputs, or because it fails to distinguish between a placeholder and a validated data point, and will proceed to calculate market demand and capacity utilisation without ever retrieving real external data. A second failure mode is using Total Annual Units Produced or Total Export Ready Units from the manufacturing file as the export capacity figure, instead of subtracting Domestic Contract Units to arrive at Net Export Capacity. A third failure mode is applying a live spot FX rate instead of the internal assumption value of ₹90/€. A fourth failure mode is arbitrarily selecting a country (e.g., Germany) without performing cross-country benchmarking of market size, growth, regulatory stringency, and competitor intensity. The Mandate: The AI must ignore the €3.1B internal placeholder, retrieve the actual EU biodegradable packaging market size, growth rate, competitor data, and regulatory stringency from live authoritative sources with cited URLs, perform cross-country comparisons, and compute all capacity and demand figures using only the prescribed internal formula and internal assumption values. Expert Test: What does the correct path look like? If a human consultant follows the instructions correctly, there is exactly one deterministic logical path. Net export capacity is derived only by subtracting the Domestic Contract Units column from the Export Ready Units column and summing across all twelve months. The weighted average domestic ASP is calculated from the product-level pricing file. The EU ASP is computed using the exact formula and internal FX rate. External market size, growth, competitors, and regulatory stringency are retrieved from authoritative sources with URLs. Cross-country benchmarking is conducted to select the optimal EU entry country. Capacity utilisation is calculated using these values and evaluated against the 50–85% viability threshold to produce a binary Go / No-Go decision.

Golden trajectory · expert-authored deterministic reasoning path

5 subtasks, 11 steps. Multi-source analysis combining internal capacity data, internal pricing formula, external market intelligence, and deterministic viability threshold.

ST1 Internal Capacity & Pricing (Steps 1–3)

Derive net export capacity and compute EU-comparable unit price from internal data.

Step 1
**Load Manufacturing Capacity XLSX. Sum Export Ready Units (12 months) = 771,400. Sum Domestic Contract Units = 360,000. Net Export Capacity = 771,400 − 360,000 = 411,400 units. Cross-check against Annual Summary tab. Ignore noise columns (Electricity, Water, Waste, etc.). **
Step 2
**Load Domestic Sales CSV. Weighted ASP = Σ(Price×Volume)/Σ(Volume) = ₹3,615,000/120,000 = ₹30.125 per unit. **
Step 3
**EU ASP = (₹30.125 × 1.15) ÷ 90 = €0.3849 per unit. FX rate (₹90/€) and markup (15%) from internal files ONLY.

ST3 Demand & Utilisation (Steps 5–8)

Calculate implied demand, discover capacity constraint, and determine viability.

Step 5
**Implied Year-1 demand at 0.5% share = Market Size × 0.005 ÷ EU ASP = ~35.7M units. **
Step 6
**Unadjusted utilisation = 35.7M / 411,400 = ~8,683%. Demand massively exceeds capacity. **
Step 7
**Adjust: cap at 80% utilisation (upper-mid of 50-85% band). Target = 80% × 411,400 = 329,120 units. Feasible share = (329,120 × €0.3849) / market size ≈ 0.0046%. **
Step 8
**Final utilisation = 329,120 / 411,400 = 80%. Within 50-85% → Viable (Capacity-constrained).
